Executive Summary

This order dataset (Jan–Mar 2024, 5 vendors, 4 warehouses) was processed through a multi-stage cleaning pipeline. The quality score improved from 45 to 73 out of 100. Three cleaning passes resolved all fixable issues: 14 duplicate rows, 6 sentinel placeholder prices, 1 type error in Quantity, and 4 malformed dates. Outliers were flagged rather than capped to preserve data integrity.

The sole remaining issue is missing values in 4 columns (12–20%), which require source system recovery and cannot be reliably imputed.

Impact: ~17–20% of orders are excluded from revenue/pricing analysis due to missing Unit_Price and Total_Amount. Missing Vendor_Name affects 12% of supplier-level reporting. Missing Ship_Date affects 17% of delivery time analysis.

Recommendations & Action Plan

1. Recover missing values from source system Effort: Medium Impact: High

Four columns have 12–20% missing data. Query the source ERP/order management system to backfill Vendor_Name, Unit_Price, Total_Amount, and Ship_Date. For Ship_Date, check if nulls correlate with PENDING or CANCELLED statuses.

2. Use outlier flag columns for downstream filtering Effort: Low Impact: Low

The 4 flagged outliers (1 negative return, 2 large orders) are already handled. Use Unit_Price_outlier_flag and Total_Amount_outlier_flag columns to exclude or include them as needed during analysis.

3. Confirm inferred Order_Dates with source records Effort: Low Impact: Low

Three dates (rows 10, 96, 120) were inferred using Ship_Date minus median lead time. Verify these against the source system if date precision is critical for time-series analysis.

4. Add ingestion-level validation rules Effort: Medium Impact: Long-term

Prevent recurrence: enforce numeric Quantity, valid YYYY-MM-DD dates, unique Order_ID, price range constraints, and reject sentinel placeholder values at the ETL layer.
